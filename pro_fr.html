2013 · Consumer Insights

Grandma's Stall: My First Lesson in Brand Storytelling

Click to expand

Long before I knew what a "Unique Selling Proposition" was, I was just a kid trying to help my grandma deal with her surplus veggies. I decided to share these great BIO products in the local market. Instead of shouting clever sales pitches, I simply scribbled the undeniable facts on a piece of cardboard: "soybean residue fertilizer," "all-natural," and "fresh from the garden."

Spoiler alert: it worked. People trusted the honesty. It was my very first, accidental lesson in consumer psychology: authentic storytelling and crystal-clear communication will always build a stronger connection than fancy marketing tricks.

2015 · Brand Positioning

The Flea Market Pop-Up

Click to expand

Who knew a weekend flea market could be a crash course in brand positioning? Catching the early wave of the Japanese stationery trend, I didn't just sell random items. I curated products with a highly consistent aesthetic and built an immersive visual display that made a simple folding table feel like a boutique concept store.

Because the stall's style stood out so clearly from the surrounding clutter, shoppers were naturally drawn in. The result? I was able to sell at a premium price and sell out incredibly fast. It was my first real-world proof that a cohesive aesthetic and clear brand positioning are powerful growth levers.
